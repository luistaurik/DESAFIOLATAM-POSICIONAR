The iguanas

Iguanas are a type of large, herbivorous lizard native to Central and South America, as well as some Caribbean islands. They are known for their distinctive appearance, with long, slender bodies, sharp claws, and a crest of spines running down their backs.

There are several different species of iguanas, ranging in size from small and agile to large and heavy-bodied. Some common species include the green iguana, the spiny-tailed iguana, and the marine iguana.
